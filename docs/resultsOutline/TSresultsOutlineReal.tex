\section*{Research questions}
% <><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><><>
\begin{enumerate}
\item Does tree growth increase with longer growing seasons?
\item Does a warmer thermal season increase growth in the following year?
\item Did an earlier start and later end of season increase growth? 
\item How did tree growth change across years and tree ids?
\end{enumerate}

  \begin{enumerate}

  \item Dataset includes 11 deciduous tree species with leaf phenology data spanning nine years (2016 to 2024) for a total of 
  321 leafout and 
  284 leaf coloring
   observations, yielding 
  278 complete growing seasons (GS) for 
  49 individuals. 
  
  \item Mean summer temperatures spanned 
  21.53$^\circ$C 
  (2017) to 
  23.07$^\circ$C 
  (2020), with an average of
  22.51$^\circ$C across the nine years.

  %emw22Jun -- fix as in wildcrokie, also why is 2016 separated out? Severe vs extreme? 
  \item 2020 and 2022 had moderate droughts, ramped up to severe in September. 
  
  \item Moderate drought in June 2016, ramped up to severe in July, then extreme in August/September. 
  
 % \item No droughts in the other years. %emw22Jun --  obvious, cut this
  
  \item GSL ranged from

% Question 1 --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- ---
\item Does tree growth increase with longer growing seasons?
  \begin{enumerate}

  \item No clear trend of growing season length effect across our eleven species and season predictors.

%emw22Jun -- we don't often say 'for no species' -- you could say 'for any species' Given your opening \item I would just change to: this did not increase growth, this did not increase growth .... 
  \item Longer thermal season increased growth for no species and decreased growth for {\Ball} (
  -0.23, UI:
  -0.36,
  -0.1 RW change per scaled GDD; Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotbspp}a and e; Table \ref{tab:ts_slope_table}).
  
  \item No species responded to longer calendar season (Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotbspp}b and f; Table \ref{tab:ts_slope_table}).

  \end{enumerate}

% Question 2 --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- ---
\item Does a warmer thermal season increase growth in the following year?
  \begin{enumerate}
  
  \item Previous year GDD increased growth for {\Ball} (
  0.15, UI:
  0.03,
  0.27 RW change per scaled GDD),
  and decreased growth for {\Bnig} (
  -0.08, UI:
  -0.16,
  0 RW change per scaled GDD; Figure \ref{fig:tsPrvsYr}b)
  
  \item Interestingly, {\Bnig} did not solely respond to current year's condition (Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotbspp}a and e; Table \ref{tab:ts_slope_table}), but grew more with warmer thermal season when the previous condition were considered (
  0.12, UI:
  0.03,
  0.21 RW change per scaled GDD; Figure \ref{fig:tsPrvsYr}a).
  \end{enumerate}
  
% Question 3 --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- ---
\item Did an earlier start and later end of season increase growth? 
%emw22Jun -- remember that with a CI of 90% then 10% of the time you'll find a species that changes even if it did not ... so if these are small effects (close to 0) I would interpret them cautiously
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item An earlier start of season increased growth for no species and decreased growth for {\Tame} (
  0.15, UI:
  0.03,
  0.27 RW change per seven SOS days; Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotbspp}c and g; Table \ref{tab:ts_slope_table}), which did not respond to the thermal or the calendar season.

\item A later end of season increased growth for no species and decreased growth for {\Ball} (
-0.09, UI:
-0.16,
-0.01 RW change per seven EOS days; Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotbspp}d and h; Table \ref{tab:ts_slope_table}), which concurrently decreased growth with warmer thermal season.

\item The estimates were unchanged when using the full or restricted datasets (SOS $n =$
321 and EOS $n =$
284 vs $n=$
278; Figure \ref{fig:tsSOSEOScomparison}b), or when using budburst instead of leafout as the start of the growing season (Figure \ref{fig:buburstVSleafoutTS}c).
\end{enumerate}

% Question 4 --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- --- ---
\item How did tree growth change across years and tree ids?
  \begin{enumerate}
  
  \item We did not find any clear effect of year on growth (Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotayear}) and there is no clear differences of growth across species (Figure \ref{fig:tsxyGDDyr} and Figure \ref{fig:tsxyGSLyr})
  
  \item Growth did not change across any tree id (Figure \ref{fig:tsmuplotatreeid}). %emw22Jun -- Do you have have spp partial pooling in this model? If so, you could compare the variance across species and tree ID a little. How different are species? How different are tree IDs compared to how different species are? (And compared to year?) ... looking at Fig S1 and S7 they look all sort of similar but I am not sure -- you can compare the sigma values. 
  %emw22Jun -- Did earlier SOS lead to earlier EOS?

  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
